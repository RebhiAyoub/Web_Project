Check Your Trimester

Trimester 1

Continue your daily physical activity for as long as you feel comfortable doing so. The more active you are during pregnancy, the easier it will be for you to adapt to your changing body. Make sure to nourish your and your baby's growing bodies with nutritious food.

Trimester 2

The second trimester marks a turning point for the mother and fetus. You will usually begin to feel better and start showing the pregnancy more. Your fetus has now developed all its organs and systems and will now begin growing in length and weight.

Trimester 3

During the third trimester, your fetus continues to grow in size and weight. The lungs are still maturing, and the fetus begins to position itself head down. By the end of the third trimester, the fetus is about 19 to 21 inches long and weighs, on average, 6 to 9 pounds.

First Trimester Tips

1-Schedule your first prenatal appointment.
2-Start taking prenatal vitamins.
3-Get regular exercise.
4-Schedule your first prenatal appointment.
5-Eat a healthy diet
6-Quit smoking and drinking alcohol.
7-Ask questions.

Second Trimester Tips

1-Limit caffeine: Caffeine can cross the placenta and affect your baby's nervous system. It is best to limit your caffeine intake to 200 milligrams per day.
2-Manage stress: Stress can have a negative impact on your baby's health. Find healthy ways to manage stress, such as yoga, meditation, or spending time in nature.
3-Get enough sleep: Most pregnant women need around 7-9 hours of sleep per night. This is because your body is working hard to support your growing baby.

Third Trimester Tips

1-Prepare for labor and delivery.
2-Be aware of the signs of labor and delivery: These include contractions that become regular and stronger, bloody show, and a bulging bag of water. If you experience any of these symptoms, call your doctor immediately.
3-Take a childbirth class.
4-Get your flu and Tdap vaccines.
5-Consider hiring a doula: A doula can provide emotional and physical support during labor and delivery.
